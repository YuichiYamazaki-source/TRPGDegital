# The Haunting (悪霊の家)

> Source: Call of Cthulhu Quick-Start Rules, 7th Edition (Chaosium Inc.)
> An introductory scenario for new Keepers and players.

## Overview

- **Setting:** 1920s Boston, Massachusetts (can be adapted to modern era)
- **Estimated play time:** 3-4 hours
- **Recommended investigators:** Private detectives, journalists, friends of the landlord
- **Key skills:** Library Use, Spot Hidden, Persuade/Charm/Fast Talk/Intimidate, Dodge, Fighting, Firearms
- **SAN checks:** Multiple (floating knife, Corbitt encounter, bed attack)

---

## Keeper's Secret

Walter Corbitt's body lies in the basement of the Corbitt House. His mind is still alive and aware of everything happening in the house. He haunts the place. Corbitt knows Cthulhu Mythos sorcery, which allowed him to preserve his consciousness after death and reanimate his body. He occasionally attacks residents in a vampire-like manner and drives away or kills anyone who discovers his secrets.

To solve the mystery, investigators must first learn about Corbitt. Meanwhile, Corbitt will notice them, try to mislead them, scare them away, and if that fails, try to kill them.

---

## Preparation

Tell the players before character creation:

> The investigators are hired to explore an old house in 1920s Boston. Rumors say it's haunted!

Players should form a team of private detectives, amateur investigators, journalists, or friends of the landlord.

---

## Player Handout 1: Introduction

> Mr. Stephen Knott recently inherited a property in central Boston known as the Corbitt House. He asks the investigators to look into the mysterious events surrounding it. The previous tenants, the Macario family, suffered terrible misfortunes. The house's reputation has made it impossible to rent or sell. Mr. Knott hopes the investigators can resolve the matter and restore the house's reputation. He offers fair compensation for their time and effort, gives them the house key, the address, and $25 as an advance payment.
>
> Before heading to the house, investigators may want to conduct some research. They can visit the Boston Globe newspaper office, the Central Library, or the Hall of Records.

---

## Location 1: Introduction

The introduction can take place anywhere appropriate — a detective's office, the landlord's home, a café. Give Player Handout 1 to the players. Allow time to discuss, plan, and decide where to go first.

**Keeper note:** If players head straight for the house, suggest they do some research first.

---

## Location 2: The Boston Globe

A daily newspaper of good repute. The reference room (basement clipping files) is not open to the public.

### Gaining Access

Investigators must convince editor **Arty Wilmot** to let them access the clipping files. Arty is pompous and will initially refuse.

**Skill check (Regular difficulty):** Charm, Intimidate, Persuade, or Fast Talk.

- **Success:** Arty grudgingly allows access.
- **Failure:** Arty smugly tells them to leave.

**Pushing the roll:** If the player wants to persist, they may push. Consequences of a pushed failure:
- Intimidate: fight breaks out
- Persuade/Charm: Arty gets angry, calls security
- In any case, Arty calls reinforcements to escort them out.

### Player Handout 2: Clipping Files

> An unpublished 1918 Boston Globe article: In 1880, a French immigrant family moved into the house but fled after terrible disasters. The parents died, and three children were left disabled. The house stood empty for a long time.
>
> In 1909, another family moved in but was soon struck by illness. In 1914, the eldest son went mad and killed himself with a kitchen knife. The heartbroken family left. In 1918, the Macario family rented the house but quickly left due to inexplicable events.

**Note:** Record keeper Ruth Blake can mention that files before an 1878 fire are incomplete. No roll needed for this.

---

## Location 3: The Central Library

Each half-day of research allows one Library Use roll per player. Failed rolls can be retried (each retry = another half-day). If research takes more than a day, Mr. Knott contacts investigators to urge progress.

Each successful Library Use roll grants one handout (in order):

### Player Handout 3
> In 1835, a prosperous merchant built the house but soon fell ill and sold it to Walter Corbitt.

### Player Handout 4
> In 1852, Walter Corbitt was sued by neighbors for "strange habits and sinister conduct," demanding he leave the area.

### Player Handout 5
> Corbitt apparently won the lawsuit. His 1866 obituary states he still lived at the same address. It also mentions a second lawsuit — neighbors tried to prevent his will's instruction to bury him in the house's basement.

### Player Handout 6
> No record of the second lawsuit's outcome exists.

---

## Location 4: Hall of Records

**Library Use roll:** Success grants Player Handout 7.

### Player Handout 7
> Civil court records show Walter Corbitt's will was executed by Reverend Michael Thomas, pastor of the "Chapel of Contemplation & Church of Our Lord Granter of Secrets." Church registers show the Chapel was closed in 1912.

If players think to check criminal court records for the Chapel, they find a 1912 trial reference but no actual records. A clerk (or successful Law roll) explains serious crimes go to county/state/federal courts.

---

## Location 5: Higher Courts / Central Police Station

At least one successful skill roll needed to access records. Players choose one:

- **Law:** Check for contacts. Success = friendly clerk Kim DeBran grants access. Failure = DeBran demands bribe then betrays them.
- **Credit Rating (75%+):** Influence the clerk.
- **Persuade:** Must provide a convincing reason.
- **Charm:** A likeable investigator might get access with a wink.
- **Fast Talk:** May need fake ID — risky in a courthouse.

**Pushing consequences:** Criminal risk, police hostility, body search.

### Player Handout 8
> Police raid on the Chapel of Contemplation: Based on sworn statements about missing neighborhood children. During the raid, 3 police officers and 17 cultists died in gunfire and fire. The coroner's report is suspiciously vague.
>
> 54 church members were arrested; all but 8 were released. Records suggest illegal intervention by local officials — the story was never published despite being the city's biggest crime case.
>
> Rev. Michael Thomas was sentenced to 40 years for 5 counts of second-degree murder. He escaped prison in 1917 and fled to another state.

---

## Location 6: The Neighborhood

Most pre-WWI residents have moved or died. The Corbitt House is the only private residence, with an overgrown front yard.

### Doherty the Shopkeeper

Roll D100 vs investigator's APP or Credit Rating to determine Doherty's attitude. If unfriendly, other investigators can try Charm/Fast Talk/Persuade/Intimidate.

**Doherty's information:**
- The Macario family moved in 2 years ago.
- After 1 year, the father had a serious accident, then went violently insane.
- He raved about a monster with burning eyes.
- About 1 month later, the mother also went insane.
- Both parents are at **Roxbury Sanitarium** (3-5km from Boston).
- The children were sent to relatives in Baltimore.

**Keeper note:** Doherty is a gossip and exaggerates. Not everything he says needs to be true.

If asked about the Chapel, Doherty points to its location 2-3 blocks away.

---

## Location 7: Roxbury Sanitarium

**Vittorio Macario** (father): Clearly insane. Clutches a Bible to his chest. May open it and point to a passage: *"By his own weapons shall the devil be destroyed!"* (Not a real Bible quote, but a clue — Corbitt's dagger can kill Corbitt. Don't over-emphasize this.)

**Gabriella Macario** (mother): Lucid and talkative. Describes a malevolent presence in the house. It would press down on her at night, throw objects when angry, and particularly hated Vittorio. She cannot provide specific details. The interview should be cut short as she becomes agitated.

**Children** (in Baltimore): Know nothing useful. They miss their parents and sometimes had nightmares about a strange man with burning eyes.

---

## Location 8: The Chapel of Contemplation

> Read aloud: The ruins of an old church sit at the end of a winding, dirty street. Weathered and overgrown, the gray rubble looks more like natural stone than building remnants. On a crumbling wall, a symbol painted in white — clearly recent — shows three Ys forming a triangle with an eye in the center.

**Player Handout 9:** Show the symbol illustration.

While near the symbol, investigators feel a throbbing sensation in their foreheads — like a headache, but different. It stops when they leave.

### Collapsing Floor

Investigators exploring the ruins may discover rotted floorboards hidden beneath the ground.

- **Luck roll:** Failure means the floor gives way.
- **Jump roll:** Success = leap to safety. Failure = fall 3m into the basement.
- **Damage:** 1D6 from the fall. Pushed Jump failure = additional consequences (broken equipment, not more damage).

### The Basement Under the Chapel

> Read aloud: The fallen investigator lands in an isolated room. The stairs that once led here are buried under tons of rubble. Near a shelf lie two skeletons in tattered silk robes — cultists who hid from police but died in the fire.

**Spot Hidden roll:** Success finds a journal and a grimoire on the shelf.
- Pushed Spot Hidden failure: step on a nail or ruin clothing.

**The Journal:** Crumbles as pages turn. Mentions Walter Corbitt was buried in the Corbitt House basement *"by his own wish and the wish of 'That Which Waits in the Dark.'"*

**The Grimoire (Book of Eibon):** Written in Latin. "First reading" takes 3+ hours (requires Other Language (Latin) roll if skill < 50%). Reading grants: +2% Cthulhu Mythos, -2 maximum SAN.

---

## Location 9: The Corbitt House

> Read aloud: The brick building is wedged between two taller, newer office buildings. The front faces the street. The back garden is overgrown with a half-collapsed awning. The house is unremarkable, almost hidden in its neighbors' shadows. Curtained windows conceal everything inside.

The front door has one lock plus 4 recently added deadbolts. Ground-floor windows are nailed shut from inside.

---

### Ground Floor

**Room 1 — Storage Room:** Full of junk (rusty water tank, old bicycle, boxes). A boarded-up cupboard contains **3 volumes of Corbitt's diary** (English, some strange expressions). Reading all 3 takes 2 full days. Grants: +4% Cthulhu Mythos, costs 1D4 SAN. Contains the spell *"Summon/Bind Dimensional Shambler"* (takes 2D6 weeks to learn after reading; not intended for use in this scenario).

**Room 2 — Second Storage Room:** Old, useless furniture.

**Room 3 — Mud Room:** Coats, boots, umbrellas, coal bags. The back door is sealed with 3 deadbolts and 2 locks.

**Room 4 — Living Room:** Radio, couch, armchair, shelves with cheap ornaments. Notably many Catholic items — crosses, Virgin Mary statues.

**Keeper note:** Build a creepy atmosphere. Corbitt haunts the house. Strange sounds from upstairs (Room 3, 2nd floor) can occur at any time.

**Room 5 — Dining Room:** Mahogany table, sideboard, 7 chairs (3 set but unused). Rotting rice soup in bowls.

**Room 6 — Kitchen:** Ice-cooled refrigerator, wood-burning stove/oven, small pantry. Some canned food, rice, pasta, homemade wine. Non-rotten food has been eaten by rats.

---

### Upper Floor

**Room 1 — Master Bedroom:** Vittorio and Gabriella's room. Double bed, bookshelf. Many crosses, candles. Rosary and prayer book on nightstand.

**Room 2 — Children's Bedroom:** Two small beds, toys, wardrobe. Airplane and cowboy pictures on walls.

**Room 3 — Spare Bedroom:** Bed frame with bare springs, empty wardrobe. This was old Corbitt's room. His spiritual influence lingers here.

**Corbitt's Actions from This Room:**
- Heavy thumping sounds (audible throughout the house)
- Blood pools on floor / blood dripping from ceiling/walls
- Rattling/scratching sounds from windows and doors
- Violently move the bed

#### The Bed Attack

Corbitt lures an investigator to check the window, then launches the bed at them.

- **Spot Hidden:** Notice the bed moving.
- **Dodge (if Spot Hidden succeeds):** Avoid the bed.
- **Failure:** Investigator is slammed through the window. Damage: **1D6+2** from broken glass and fall.
- **SAN check (witnessing the bed move):** 1/1D4

**Keeper note:** If an investigator dies, have Mr. Knott accompany the group and give the dead investigator's player control of Knott.

**Room 4 — Bathroom:** Sink, bathtub, flush toilet. Towels for 4 people. Bathtub has brackish water from a leaky faucet.

---

### Basement

The basement door is locked with 1 key and 3 deadbolts (opens only from upstairs). Stairs are poorly repaired; lights don't work (Corbitt cut the breaker in the kitchen).

#### Descending the Stairs

Each investigator rolls **DEX + Climb** (one D100 roll compared to both). Both failures = warned the stairs are dangerous. Can stay on the ground floor or push the roll. Pushed failure = fall to basement floor, **1D6 damage**.

**Keeper note:** An investigator who already succeeded can help others (grants 1 bonus die, but failure means both fall).

**Room 1 — Storage Room:** Tools, pipes, trash can lids, wood, nails, screws scattered about.

**Spot Hidden (difficult clue):** Find Corbitt's enchanted knife. Push failure = cut hand on the knife, **1D4+2 damage**.

The walls are wooden panels. Even a cursory look reveals hollow spaces behind them (Rooms 2 and 3).

#### The Floating Knife

An old knife with an ornate handle and thick "rust" (actually dried blood). This is Corbitt's magical dagger.

**If found:** The knife levitates and attacks.
**If not found:** The knife attacks when investigators start breaking through walls.

**Knife Attack (each combat round, costs Corbitt 1 MP):**

| Corbitt's Roll vs POW 90 | Investigator's Dodge | Result |
|---------------------------|---------------------|--------|
| Failure (91+) | — | Miss |
| Regular (46-90) | Failure | **1D4+2 damage** |
| Hard (19-45) | Failure or Regular | **1D4+2 damage** |
| Extreme (1-18) | Failure, Regular, or Hard | **Impale: 6+1D4+2 damage** |

- Grabbing a trash can lid for defense: **1 bonus die on Dodge**
- If unaware of the knife: Spot Hidden to notice it. Failure = no Dodge allowed, attacker gets 1 bonus die.
- **SAN check (seeing floating knife):** 1/1D4

**Combat Maneuver to Grab the Knife:** Use Fighting (Brawl) vs Corbitt's POW in opposed roll. Build comparison is ignored (no one physically holds the knife). Thick coat = 1 bonus die.

**If an investigator holds the knife:** Corbitt may try to take it back (costs 1 additional MP per round). Opposed roll: investigator's STR vs Corbitt's POW.

**Room 2 — Empty Storage:** Originally for coal. Coal chute door is nailed shut.

**Room 3 — Corbitt's Hidden Passage:** Breaking through wooden walls reveals a narrow gap. Rat nest inside, foul smell.

**Rat Swarm:** If not given an escape route, rats attack anyone entering (combat maneuver: swarm).

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| STR 35 | CON 55 | SIZ 35 | POW 50 | DEX 70 |
| HP: 9 | DB: -1 | Build: -1 | Move: 9 |
| Fighting: 40% (Hard 20% / Extreme 8%) | Damage: 1D3 |
| Swarm (combat maneuver): Damage 2D6 (1 bonus die on attack) |
| Dodge: 42% |

Killing even 1-2 rats causes the rest to flee.

Wall inscription: *"Chapel of Contemplation"* carved roughly — a clear clue (no Spot Hidden needed).

Breaking through this wall leads to Room 4.

---

### Room 4: Corbitt's Lair

> Read aloud: A motionless figure lies on a crude bed in the center of the room. About 180cm tall, withered like wood. Nothing but skin and bones, with unnervingly wide-open saucer-like eyes and a knife-sharp nose. Something chain-like hangs around its neck. Completely hairless, with abnormally long teeth from receded gums. A nauseating sweet smell emanates from the body — like rotting corn.
>
> The dirt floor has a table in the southwest corner with a rolled-up piece of paper on it.

The paper crumbles on touch. It appears to be an astrological chart — a potential hook for future adventures.

#### Corbitt's Dominate Spell

Corbitt can cast Dominate at any time (costs 1 MP, instant casting, uses DEX+50 for initiative). One investigator per casting; target must be inside the Corbitt House.

**Opposed roll:** Corbitt's POW 90 vs investigator's POW.

**Success (Corbitt wins):** Target is dazed for 1D6+1 rounds under Corbitt's telepathic control. Can force murder or reckless actions (not suicide). Victim remembers nothing afterward.

#### Corbitt Rises

Costs 2 MP for 5 combat rounds of movement. Corbitt only moves when threatened.

**SAN check (seeing Corbitt rise):** 1/1D8

If 5+ SAN lost: INT roll. Success = temporary insanity.

**Bouts of Madness:** Roll 1D10, consult the Bouts of Madness table. Add a phobia/mania or modify a backstory entry.

#### Combat with Corbitt

Initiative by DEX order. Corbitt's DEX = 35 (DEX 85 when casting spells with +50).

- Investigators with drawn firearms: DEX + 50 for initiative.
- Corbitt fights back with Fighting (Brawl) against melee attacks.
- Firearms: just roll to hit. At close range, 1 bonus die.
- Second+ attacker gets 1 bonus die (outnumbered rule).

## Walter Corbitt — Undead Sorcerer

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| STR | 90 |
| CON | 115 |
| SIZ | 55 |
| INT | 80 |
| POW | 90 |
| DEX | 35 |
| APP | 5 |
| EDU | 80 |
| SAN | 0 (ignores SAN costs for spells) |
| HP | 17 |
| DB | +1D4 |
| Build | 1 |
| Move | 8 |
| MP | 18 (recovers 1 per hour) |

**Attacks (1 per round):**
- Fighting: 50% (Hard 25% / Extreme 10%)
- Damage: 1D3 + 1D4 (DB) + infection risk
- Dodge: 17% (Hard 8% / Extreme 3%)

**Infection from claws:** After a successful claw attack, investigator rolls Luck.
- Luck failure: After 1 day, consciousness becomes hazy. Must roll CON.
  - CON failure: delirium for 1D10 days, lose 1D10 CON permanently.
  - CON success: delirium for 1D6 days, no further CON loss.
  - Repeat until recovery or death. Lost CON does not recover.

**Armor:** Flesh Protection spell. Roll 2D6 for armor points. Armor depletes as it absorbs damage. Cannot be recast until depleted. Lasts 24 hours.

**Floating Dagger:** See "The Floating Knife" section. Costs 1 MP per round.

**Spells:** Dominate (custom version), Flesh Protection, Summon/Bind Dimensional Shambler

**Skills:** Cthulhu Mythos 17%, Intimidate 64%, Listen 60%, Sleight of Hand 30%, Stealth 72%

**SAN loss (seeing Corbitt move):** 1/1D8

**Special weakness:** If stabbed with his own magical dagger, Corbitt instantly crumbles to dust regardless of any active spells.

**Sunlight:** Causes pain, impairs vision. May kill him (Keeper's discretion).

**Roleplay notes:**
- Corbitt is malicious; tries to divide investigators and turn them against each other.
- He takes perverse pleasure in their suffering.
- He will try to acquire any Mythos knowledge brought into the house.
- Track Corbitt's MP carefully (recovers 1/hour; Flesh Protection costs are pre-paid).

---

## Conclusion

**Victory:** If investigators destroy Corbitt, Mr. Knott pays generously. Each investigator gains **1D6 SAN**. They may claim the worm-eaten Book of Eibon from the Chapel.

**Failure (report nothing wrong):** Mr. Knott stays overnight in the house. Corbitt kills him with the dagger. Investigators face police investigation and must prove their innocence.

**Death/Madness:** Some investigators may die or go insane. Keeper narrates brief epilogues for each.

### Future Hooks
- The recently painted symbol on the Chapel ruins suggests ongoing cult activity.
- The mysterious astrological chart on Corbitt's table may lead to further adventures.
- The 1912 cover-up hints at a larger conspiracy.
